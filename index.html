Why average molecular weight?

We know that in polymerisation large numbers of chain reactions are propagating that's why instead of using absolute molecular weight we use average molecular weight for accurate calculations of properties

Molecular Weight's significance in Polymers

The molecular weight has a significant effect on the mechanical and physical bulk properties of polymers. A higher molecular weight improves the mechanical properties, i.e break, yield, and impact strength increase. However, a higher molecular weight also increases the melt and glass transition temperature as well as the solution and melt viscosity which makes processing and forming of the polymeric material more difficult.
